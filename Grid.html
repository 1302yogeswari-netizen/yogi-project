The Universe

The universe is the vast space that contains everything that exists, including matter, energy, space, and time. It consists of billions of galaxies such as the Milky Way, each containing billions of stars. One of these stars is the Sun, which forms the center of our solar system. Around the Sun revolve planets such as Earth, along with their natural satellites like the Moon.

The universe also contains other celestial bodies such as asteroids, comets like Halley's Comet, meteoroids, interstellar gas, and cosmic dust. In addition, scientists believe that a large part of the universe is made of invisible components called dark matter and dark energy. Thus, the universe includes galaxies, stars, planetary systems, planets, moons, and many other celestial objects that together form the cosmic structure.
